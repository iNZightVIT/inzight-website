---
title: Identify Points
description: Label and identify specific points in your plots.
---

The **Identify Points** options in **Add to Plot** allow you to label, colour, and highlight specific points of interest in scatter plots. This is useful for investigating outliers, highlighting specific observations, or adding context to your visualisations.

## Labelling points

You can label points on a scatter plot by selecting a variable to use as labels from the **Identify** dropdown. The label variable determines what text appears next to each identified point â€” typically a name, ID, or category.

When no label variable is selected but points are identified, row numbers are shown instead.

## Selection methods

iNZight provides several ways to choose which points to identify:

### By extreme values

Select the most **extreme points** based on their Mahalanobis distance from the centre of the data. This automatically identifies the points that are most unusual in terms of both their x and y values considered together. You can control how many extreme points to identify (e.g., the top 5 or 10 most extreme).

### By row ID

Specify particular observations to label by providing their row numbers. This is useful when you already know which observations you want to highlight (e.g., from a previous analysis or data inspection).

### By expression

Identify points that satisfy a logical condition (e.g., `x > 100` or `height > 180`). This lets you highlight all points meeting a specific criterion.

### Same level identification

When identifying points, you can also highlight all observations that share the same level of a grouping variable as the identified points. For example, if you identify an extreme point and it belongs to group "A", all points in group "A" can be highlighted together.

## Colouring identified points

Identified points can be drawn in a distinct colour (default: **red**) to make them stand out from the rest of the data. When combined with labels, this creates a clear visual emphasis on the points of interest.

## How it works

- Identified points are labelled with text from the selected variable
- Labels are positioned near their corresponding points
- Non-identified points remain in their default colour
- The identified points are drawn as solid filled points for emphasis

## Applicable plot types

Point identification is available for **scatter plots** only, as other plot types do not display individual data points in the same way.
